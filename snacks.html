Enhancing Snack Discovery on Snacks.com

Redesigned the Snacks.com mobile experience to improve product discovery and increase cart value among college students through incentive-driven, social, and curated features.

Role: UX Designer. I was responsible for mapping information architecture and prioritizing impactful web pages, consumer journey mapping, initial concept sketching, making interactive prototypes using Figma, user testing, and iteration.
Timeline: 14 weeks
Team: 6 designers, 2 PepsiCo designers (mentors)
Deliverables: Research, wireframes, prototypes

Problem Framing

Snacks.com functions as a standard e-commerce platform, limiting engagement and failing to differentiate from grocery or delivery apps. College students—high-frequency snack consumers—primarily discover snacks through social media but purchase elsewhere.

User Group: College students (mobile-first, budget-conscious, socially influenced)

How might we:

  • Encourage users to explore and discover new snacks?
  • Increase cart value without relying solely on discounts?
  • Support social and group-based snacking behaviors?

Munch Meter

Question: How might we incentivize users to add more to their cart without being intrusive?

Method: Co-design workshop, think-aloud testing

Finding: Users respond to visible progress toward rewards but dislike pushy UI.

Design Outcome: A collapsible progress-based reward meter that tracks incentives like free shipping.

Impact: Encourages higher cart value through subtle behavioral nudging.

Collaborative Variety Pack Builder

Question: How might we support shared snack purchasing?

Method: Co-design workshop, journey mapping

Finding: Snack planning is social but fragmented across tools.

Design Outcome: A shareable pack builder enabling multiple contributors.

Impact: Reduces coordination friction and supports real-world behavior.

Bundles & Themed Packs

Question: How might we reduce choice overload?

Method: Survey, think-aloud testing

Finding: Users prefer curated, value-driven bundles tied to occasions.

Design Outcome: Pre-built themed snack packs with savings highlighted.

Impact: Simplifies decisions and increases cart size.

Overall Impact

Increased engagement through interactive and social features

Encouraged higher cart value via rewards and bundling

Reduced decision fatigue through guided experiences

Improved differentiation from traditional e-commerce platforms

Reflection

Simplicity over flexibility: Users preferred guided experiences over full customization.

Designing for behavior: Grounding decisions in real behaviors led to stronger outcomes.

Future scope: Validate with higher fidelity testing and measure retention and repeat use.
